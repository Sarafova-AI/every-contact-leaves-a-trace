Factors that favor open carotid endarterectomy over carotid stenting include:

1. Anatomical suitability: Open carotid endarterectomy may be more suitable for patients with certain anatomical variations or complex plaques that are not easily accessible for stenting.

2. Long-term durability: Carotid endarterectomy has been shown to have a lower risk of restenosis compared to carotid stenting, making it a more durable option for some patients.

3. Patient age and comorbidities: Older patients or those with multiple comorbidities may be better candidates for carotid endarterectomy due to lower procedural risks compared to stenting.

4. Cost considerations: In some healthcare systems, carotid endarterectomy may be a more cost-effective option compared to carotid stenting, especially in patients with lower procedural risks.

5. Surgeon expertise: Carotid endarterectomy may be preferred in centers with experienced vascular surgeons who have a high success rate and low complication rates with the procedure.
