1. Australia:
Australia has a well-established regulatory framework for clinical trials, including those involving pediatric populations. In order to conduct a pediatric HPV vaccine trial in Australia, researchers would need to submit a Clinical Trial Notification (CTN) or Clinical Trial Exemption (CTX) application to the TGA, as well as seek approval from a Human Research Ethics Committee (HREC). The trial would also need to comply with the Australian National Health and Medical Research Council (NHMRC) guidelines for research involving children.

2. China:
In China, the regulatory authority for clinical trials is the National Medical Products Administration (NMPA). Researchers would need to submit an Investigational New Drug (IND) application to the NMPA in order to conduct a pediatric HPV vaccine trial in China. The trial would also need to be approved by an Ethics Committee. China has specific regulations for pediatric clinical trials, including requirements for consent from both the child and their legal guardian.

3. Japan:
In Japan, the Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency (PMDA) is the regulatory authority for clinical trials. Researchers would need to submit a Clinical Trial Notification (CTN) or Investigator Initiated Trial (IIT) application to the PMDA in order to conduct a pediatric HPV vaccine trial in Japan. The trial would also need to be approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB). Japan has specific regulations for pediatric clinical trials, including requirements for age-appropriate informed consent.

4. South Korea:
In South Korea, the Ministry of Food and Drug Safety (MFDS) is responsible for regulating clinical trials. Researchers would need to submit a Clinical Trial Authorization (CTA) application to the MFDS in order to conduct a pediatric HPV vaccine trial in South Korea. The trial would also need to be approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB). South Korea has specific regulations for pediatric clinical trials, including requirements for assent from the child, in addition to consent from their legal guardian.

Overall, while there are similarities in the regulatory requirements for pediatric clinical trials across APAC countries, there are also significant differences in terms of the specific regulatory agencies involved, the types of applications required, and the specific guidelines for pediatric research. Researchers planning a pediatric HPV vaccine trial in the APAC region would need to carefully review and comply with the specific regulatory requirements of each country in which they plan to conduct the trial.
