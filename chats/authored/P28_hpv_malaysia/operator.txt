In Southeast Asia, the landscape for HPV vaccination for pediatric use ages 8 to 12 is still developing. While many countries in the region have included the HPV vaccine in their national immunization schedules for adolescent girls aged 9 to 13, there is limited focus on vaccinating younger children in the 8 to 12 age group.

Other countries, like Thailand and the Philippines, have started to include the HPV vaccine in their national immunization schedules, but coverage rates vary and access to vaccination services can be limited in rural areas.

Efforts are being made to increase awareness about the importance of HPV vaccination for preventing cervical cancer and other HPV-related diseases in both girls and boys. However, challenges such as cost, infrastructure, and vaccine hesitancy still exist in the region.

Overall, the HPV vaccine landscape for pediatric use ages 8 to 12 in Southeast Asia is still evolving, with efforts being made to expand access and coverage to younger age groups. Collaboration between governments, healthcare providers, and international organizations will be crucial in driving progress and ensuring that more children can benefit from the protection offered by the HPV vaccine.
