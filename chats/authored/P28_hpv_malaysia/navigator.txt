Country-by-Country Summary
Thailand
National Program: HPV vaccine is included in the national immunization schedule for girls, often linked to school-based programs. Boys are generally not covered by the government program but may receive HPV vaccination through private sector.

Approval: Multiple HPV vaccines (e.g., Gardasil, Cervarix) are licensed and available.

Reimbursement: The vaccine is provided free of charge to girls in the target ages as part of the national vaccine program, but not reimbursed for boys (out-of-pocket or private insurance for males).

Vietnam
National Program: Vietnam has introduced HPV vaccine in demonstration projects and regional rollouts for girls, but there is not yet a full national program. Boys are not covered.

Approval: Vaccines are approved and available, especially in urban centers, but not broadly funded.

Reimbursement: Primarily self-paid or funded by projects; not free nationwide for either sex.

Philippines
National Program: HPV vaccination is routinely provided to girls aged 9–14 as part of the government’s school-based immunization program. Boys are not covered by the national program.

Approval: Licensed vaccines are available.

Reimbursement: Government covers girls; boys must pay out-of-pocket.

Indonesia
National Program: Government program targets girls; no national, funded HPV vaccination for boys.

Approval: Approved vaccines are available for both sexes, but coverage is limited to girls under state program.

Reimbursement: Provided free to girls (e.g., school campaigns). Boys: out-of-pocket/private insurance.

South Korea
National Program: HPV vaccination is provided to girls; a pilot or expanded program for boys is not yet part of the national strategy, although the vaccine is approved for both sexes.

Approval: Multiple HPV vaccines are approved for both boys and girls aged 9+.

Reimbursement: National health insurance covers girls. Boys: self-funded.

Malaysia
National Program: National HPV program provides free vaccination to 13-year-old girls as part of the school program. Boys are not included.

Approval: Approved for both sexes, available in private sector.

Reimbursement: Government funds for girls; boys pay privately.

China
National Program: No nationwide HPV vaccination program for either sex; some cities or provinces provide funded vaccination for girls.

Approval: Multiple HPV vaccines are approved, including both imported and domestic options for ages 9+ and both sexes.

Reimbursement: Variable. Some local governments fund girls’ vaccination, almost always self-paid for boys.

Japan
National Program: After a hiatus, active government recommendation for girls resumed in 2022; catch-up efforts ongoing. Boys are not yet included in the national immunization program, although vaccine is approved for males.

Approval: Vaccines (including Gardasil, Cervarix) are approved for boys and girls.

Reimbursement: Vaccination is free for eligible girls. Boys pay out-of-pocket.

Key Insights for Trial Feasibility
Only Girls Regularly Covered: Across APAC, government-supported HPV vaccination is almost exclusively for girls. Boys are rarely included in state programs.

Vaccines Approved for Both Sexes: In all these countries, major HPV vaccines are approved for both boys and girls, even though only girls are routinely targeted for public reimbursement.

Boys = Private Market: Recruitment of boys will rely on private clinics, out-of-pocket payment by families, or research/demonstration projects. This can require additional feasibility checks and educational campaigns but does not face regulatory blocks for product access or ethical review.

No National Funding for Boys: There are minimal, if any, reimbursement schemes for boys in these countries—trials may need to factor in compensation or free vaccination as part of the study offering to parents.
