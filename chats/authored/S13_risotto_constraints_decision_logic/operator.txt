Give a straightforward mushroom risotto recipe for four people using vegetable stock and no wine.



+Assistant
Ingredients:
- 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
- 4 cups vegetable stock
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 8 oz mushrooms, sliced (cremini or button mushrooms work well)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:
1. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
2. Add the mushrooms to the pan and cook until they start to release their juices and brown, about 8-10 minutes.
3. Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir to coat with the oil, onion, and mushrooms. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice is slightly toasted.
4. Add 1 cup of vegetable stock to the pan and stir constantly until the liquid is absorbed. Continue to add the vegetable stock, 1 cup at a time, stirring frequently until the rice is creamy and cooked al dente, about 20-25 minutes.
5. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
6. Remove the pan from the heat and let it sit for a few minutes to thicken.

Practical tips for a perfect mushroom risotto:

1. Mushroom texture: To achieve the best texture for your mushrooms in the risotto, make sure to slice them evenly and not too thick. Cook the mushrooms over medium heat until they start to brown and release their juices, but be careful not to overcook them as they can become mushy. The goal is to have mushrooms that are tender with a slight bite to them.

2. Stock addition: When adding the vegetable stock to the risotto, make sure to heat it in a separate saucepan and keep it simmering. Adding warm stock to the risotto helps maintain a consistent cooking temperature and prevents the rice from becoming gummy. Add the stock gradually, stirring frequently, allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding the next.

3. Finishing with butter or cheese: For a creamy and luxurious finish to your mushroom risotto, consider adding a knob of butter or a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese right at the end of cooking. This adds richness and depth of flavor to the dish. Once you've removed the risotto from the heat, stir in the butter or cheese until melted and well incorporated. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed before serving. Optional: You can also garnish the finished risotto with some additional Parmesan cheese and freshly chopped parsley for a touch of freshness.
