That's a very insightful observation about decision fatigue. While food delivery may initially seem like the easier option, it can actually introduce a new layer of decision-making complexity into our daily lives. With food delivery, every meal becomes a fresh marketplace decision, requiring us to compare restaurants, prices, delivery times, cravings, ratings, and more. This constant barrage of choices can lead to decision fatigue, where the mental effort of making these decisions becomes overwhelming and exhausting.

On the other hand, a boring home routine, such as having a set meal plan or a few go-to recipes, can actually provide a sense of freedom and relief from decision-making. When the decision is already mostly made, there is less mental effort required, allowing us to focus on other aspects of our lives without the added stress of choosing what to eat for every meal.

By recognizing the role of decision fatigue in our food choices, we can explore ways to simplify our meal planning and preparation process, whether that involves establishing a meal plan, prepping ingredients in advance, or embracing the convenience of frozen staples or batch-cooked meals. Finding a balance between convenience and decision-making can help alleviate the mental burden associated with meal choices and promote a more sustainable and enjoyable approach to food consumption.
