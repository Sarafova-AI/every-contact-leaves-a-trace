To make a lower-calorie fried turbot that still tastes delicious, follow these step-by-step instructions:

1. Begin by seasoning four turbot fillets (approximately 6 oz each) with your favorite herbs and spices.

2. Prepare a shallow dish with a mixture of 1/2 cup of breadcrumbs and 1 tsp of paprika for coating the fillets.

3. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. The oil depth should be around 1/4 inch to ensure even cooking without excessive oil absorption.

4. Dredge each fillet in the breadcrumb mixture, making sure to coat both sides evenly.

5. Carefully place the coated fillets in the hot skillet and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and crispy.

6. Once cooked, transfer the fillets to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

7. Serve the fried turbot with a squeeze of lemon and a side of steamed vegetables for a complete and satisfying meal.

By following these steps, you can enjoy a lower-calorie version of fried turbot that still retains the delicious flavor and crispy texture of traditional fried fish.
